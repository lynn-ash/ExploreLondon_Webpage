    This is purely based on the preferences of the traveler. There are plenty of benefits for traveling in either the winter or summer months.

    Winter Months:

    Those who choose to come during the winter will be welcomed with a cheerfully festive atmosphere. Because it is the holiday season, the city is decked out in all sorts of bright lights, decorations, and Christmas trees. The best areas to see these would be places such as Oxford Street, Regent Street, or Convent Garden. This is also the time of year to experience the wonders of traditional Christmas markets. These markets are stocked with delicious treats, handcrafted gifts, and different wines. There is also the benefit of smaller crowds, which provides a quiter atmosphere and makes it easier to get into major tourist attractions such as the British Museum, Buckingham Palace, or the Tower of London.


    The atmosphere also allows for quite the xozy experience. Because the weather is so cold outside, tourists can enjoy a series of different indoor treats. For instance, London has a wide range of historic cozy pubs and cafes. There, tourists can enjoy the hearty British atmosphere and hot drinks all while relaxing next to a sizzling fireplace.


    Summer Months:

    Because the weather is so warm, there are plenty of outdoor activites for tourists to choose from. For instance, the outdoor theaters such as Shakespeare's Golden Theatre or Regent Park's Open Air Theatre give people the opportunity to experience their favorite classical plays being brought to life. In addition to that, these months are when music festivals start happening. Some popular ones include the British Summer Time Hyde Park or Lovebox Festival. The weather also draws people towards the many blooming gardens London has to offer. Place such as Kew Gardens, Hyde Park, and St. James's Park serve as the perfect places to take in the vibrant and colorful scenery all while enjoying a nice picnic.

    For those who enjoy a more lively atmosphere, London becomes the central hub for tourists when it comes to shopping, dining, and sightseeing. It is during these months that outdoor dining makes a return, which allows tourists to take in some fresh air all while enjoying their meal.

    In total, there are 9 different travel zones in London:

    • Zone 1: This area is Central London, and it includes major tourist attractions such as Big Ben and the Tower of London.

    • Zone 2: Known as a cultural hub, and is home to many noteable areas. Some include Notting Hill, Camden Town, and the London Zoo.

    • Zone 3: An area that brings out London's natural beauty with places like the Kew Gardens. For those who enjoy sports, this areas is also home to the Wimbledon tennis championships. The London City Airport also resides in this zone, making transportation to and from the airport relatively simple.

    • Zone 4: This region is a bit further out, and is where places like Wembley Stadium, RAF Musuem, and Richmond Park are located. In this zone, there are also more suburban neighborhooods.

    • Zone 5: Includes more suburban areas like Croydon and Harrow. It is also the termination point for many train lines.

    • Zone 6: The outer suburban in the East and South East areas of London.

    • Zones 7-9: The last three zones are home the the outermost areas of London. This includes both some suburban and semi-rural counties.
